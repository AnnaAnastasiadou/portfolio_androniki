About Me

Androniki Charalambous

Architect | Researcher | Designer

Background

I am a 24-year-old architect from Cyprus, graduated with honours from TU Delft, following a Bachelor's degree from the Welsh School of Architecture (WSA). I am driven by a fascination with how spaces shape the emotional and social fabric of our lives. I see architecture as a facilitator of social resilience — a field through which connection, health, and sustainability can quietly emerge from the way we design, inhabit, and remember places.

Creative Approach

My work moves between research, storytelling, and design, often drawing on anthropological methods and collective narratives to understand the subtle interactions between people and their environments. Each project begins as a question rather than a form — an exploration of how architecture can strengthen relationships, preserve memory, and create continuity within change. Through programmatic design, crafted spatial sequences, and sensitivity to heritage, I seek to create spaces that feel both grounded and transformative with the intention of cultivating connection and community.

Personal Interests

Outside of architecture, I explore creativity through painting, printing, and collaging, and I enjoy music, movies, and other forms of storytelling. My personal interests, alongside my professional practice, reflect a curiosity about the human experience and the ways in which art, culture, and design intersect to shape the world around us.
